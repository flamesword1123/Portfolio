Experience

Outside of school I am in the Lang Science Program at the American Museum of Natural History. The program lasts seven years and began before the start of middle school and continues through high school. It meets every other saturday during the school year and three weeks over the summer. A variety of subjects are taught including astrophysics, anthropology, topics in biology, and more. During my time there I have been able to work on a number of research projects with scientists at the museum and my peers in the program. I also am taking an introductory biology course through edX taught by Eric Lander, a professor at MIT and the director of the Broad Institute.
